PUNJABI KABADDI

Punjabi kabaddi, also called circle style kabaddi,[1] is a contact sport that originated in the Punjab region, in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent. There are a number of traditional Punjabi kabaddi styles traditionally played in the Punjab region. Like standard kabaddi, circle style kabaddi is played at state and international levels,[2] through various governing bodies such as Kabaddi World Cup (Circle style).

The term kabaddi may be derived from the Punjabi word "kauddi" (Punjabi: ਕੌਡੀ) which is chanted to play kabaddi or, it is derived from "katta" (calf) (Punjabi: ਕੱਟਾ) and "vaddi" (to chopped) (Punjabi: ਵੱਢੀ) which together has become kabaddi.


Traditional Punjabi kabaddi styles
Lambi kauddi ਲੰਬੀ ਕੌਡੀ 15 players with a circular pitch of 15–20 feet There is no referee. The raider will say "kauddi, kauddi" throughout the attack.
Saunchi kauddi ਸੌਂਚੀ ਕੌਡੀ unlimited players with a circular playing pitch In sauchi kabaddi, the raider will hit the defender but only on the chest. The defender will then hold the raiders wrist.
Goongi kabaddi ਗੂੰਗੀ ਕਬੱਡੀ where a raider player does not speak and say the word kabaddi The struggle will continue till he reaches the starting line or acknowledge the defeat and loses a point
Other traditional styles ਛੈ ਹੰਧੀ, ਸ਼ਮਿਆਲੀ ਵਾਲੀ, ਪੀਰ ਕੌਡੀ, ਪੜ ਕੋੋੋਡੀ, ਬਧੀ, ਬੈਠਵੀ, ਬੁਰਜੀਆ ਵਾਲੀ popular in Majha area of Punjab; Also played in some parts of Pakistan
